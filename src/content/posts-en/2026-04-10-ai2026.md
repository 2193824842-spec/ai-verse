## Scoring Dimensions

### General Capability (30%)

Performance on reasoning, logic, math, code, and general knowledge benchmarks. Based on public tests (MMLU, MATH, HumanEval) plus real-world usage. Highest weight because it sets the ceiling — a weak general model can't be redeemed by other strengths.

### Chinese Language Quality (25%)

Specifically evaluates Chinese-language performance: accurate understanding of ambiguous Chinese expressions, natural-sounding output, handling of cultural context and idioms. Often more important to domestic users than raw benchmark scores — a model with strong English but stiff Chinese feels awkward to use daily.

### Free Tier & Value (20%)

How much can the free version actually do? Daily free conversation quota, whether premium models are locked, and paid tier pricing. For most individual users, "can I use this without paying?" is a real decision factor.

### Response Speed & Stability (15%)

Time-to-first-token, speed on long outputs, whether it lags during peak hours, and service reliability. Slow or unreliable tools break workflow, which directly affects daily experience.

### Scenario Breadth (10%)

Multimodal support (images, files, voice), plugins or web browsing, and vertical-scenario optimizations (code, PPT, search). Lowest weight overall, but potentially decisive for users with specific needs.

## 2026 Trend Summary

A few patterns emerge from reviewing this ranking:

**1. A two-horse race has formed**

Doubao and DeepSeek have pulled decisively ahead on both user count and technical capability. Other products aren't without value, but they can't challenge these two in general scenarios. Future competition will likely shift to verticals — whoever dominates a specific niche can hold their user base.

**2. Free is table stakes; paid thresholds are dropping**

In 2026, free tiers of mainstream Chinese AI tools are sufficient for most individual users' daily needs. Paid tiers differentiate mainly on: higher call frequency, premium model versions, and more storage. For individuals, start free — only upgrade when you genuinely hit limits.

**3. Multimodal is now baseline; vertical tools are diverging**

Image understanding, file processing, and web search have moved from "premium feature" to "standard configuration." Real differentiation is now in verticals: Kling AI for video, Jimeng AI for images, Xinghuo for voice — these specialized tools exceed general tools in their own domains.

**4. Reasoning capability is the next battleground**

DeepSeek-R1's success proved the value of "slow-thinking" reasoning models. In 2026, every major player is building reasoning models. For users, this means AI will become increasingly reliable on complex problems — but also means adapting to "waiting for it to think." Speed vs. depth will become a new dimension when choosing tools.

**5. Ecosystem integration is the trend**

The era of standalone AI tools is passing. Doubao + CapCut, Tongyi + DingTalk, ERNIE Bot + Baidu Search — ecosystem integration means a tool's value depends not just on the model itself, but on how many of your existing tools it connects to. When choosing, ask "how does this fit into my current workflow?" — that's more practical than pure model comparison.

## FAQ

**Q1: DeepSeek or Doubao — which should I choose?**

Depends on your primary use. DeepSeek for tasks requiring deep thinking: writing code, doing analysis, solving complex problems — its reasoning is stronger, free version has no notable limits. Doubao for high-frequency daily use: quick Q&A, Chinese writing, content creation — faster response, smoother Chinese experience. Both are free — use both and switch by context.

**Q2: Is the free tier of Chinese AI tools good enough, or do I need to pay?**

For most individual users, free is plenty. DeepSeek web version has no limits; Doubao core features are free; Kimi has a daily free quota. Paid plans mainly matter for: high-frequency API calls (developers), access to the latest flagship models, or genuinely hitting conversation limits. Try free for 3–7 days — only pay when you have a clear specific need.

**Q3: Is my data safe with these tools?**

A reasonable concern. General advice: don't input real names, ID numbers, bank card numbers, or other sensitive personal information into any AI tool. For work files with trade secrets, anonymize before uploading. Every platform has a privacy policy, but real-world enforcement varies. For enterprise users with compliance requirements, use private deployment versions or dedicated enterprise APIs.

**Q4: Is Kimi's long-document processing really better than alternatives?**

In terms of accuracy, yes. Kimi's key-point extraction and summarization accuracy on ultra-long documents leads the domestic market. Note: processing speed for long documents is slow; peak hours may involve queuing. If your documents are under 100,000 characters, DeepSeek and Doubao handle them fine — the gap isn't huge. For documents over 500,000 characters, Kimi is the safer choice.

**Q5: Kling AI or Jimeng AI — which is more worth it?**

Depends on need. For video, Kling AI — its video quality and "synchronized audio-video" feature is currently unmatched domestically. For images, Jimeng AI — better Chinese prompt understanding and smoother CapCut workflow integration. If you need both, register a free account for each and switch as needed.

**Q6: How big is the gap between Chinese AI tools and ChatGPT or Claude?**

The gap is much smaller in 2026 than it was in 2023. DeepSeek's reasoning performance is in the same tier as GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonnet; Doubao's Chinese-language experience is arguably better than international products for Chinese-language tasks. Main remaining gaps: multimodal capability (international products more mature), plugin ecosystem (international products richer), English content handling (international products stronger). If your primary needs are Chinese-language, domestic tools are fully sufficient — no need to deal with VPN hassle just to use ChatGPT.

## What Does "Completely Free" Actually Mean? Our Criteria

### Three Requirements Every Tool Must Meet

Every tool in this guide must satisfy all three of the following:

1. **No credit card required**: Sign up and start using — no payment info needed
2. **No trial period**: Not "free for 14 days" — a permanently free tier that doesn't expire
3. **Core features genuinely usable**: The free version can handle real work, not just a demo

### What Gets Excluded

The following types of tools are not covered here:

- **ChatGPT Free**: GPT-4o has strict daily usage limits that can be tightened at any time — not reliable enough
- **Midjourney**: Free trial has been removed; a paid subscription is now required
- **Claude.ai Free**: Daily message limits are extremely low — practically unusable for heavy users
- **Notion AI**: The base Notion is free, but AI features cost extra
- **"Limited-time free" tools**: Once the promotion ends, so does the free access

### The Real Limitations of Free Tools

Completely free doesn't mean no limitations at all. Here's an honest look at common constraints:

- **Rate limits**: Slower responses during peak hours; some tools have daily request caps
- **Context length**: Free tiers typically support shorter conversation contexts than paid plans
- **Feature caps**: Advanced features (stronger models, more storage) usually require payment
- **Stability**: Free users get lower server priority, so performance can dip when servers are busy

Understanding these trade-offs helps you plan your usage realistically — and keeps you from being misled by the word "free."

## Free vs. Paid: When Is It Worth Upgrading?

A fully free tool stack covers the majority of everyday needs — but here are situations where paying actually makes sense:

**1. You use it constantly**: If you're generating large amounts of content every day, rate limits on free plans will become a real bottleneck. Paid plans offer priority queues and higher limits that are worth the investment.

**2. You need more powerful models**: Free tiers usually run smaller or older models. For professional use cases that demand high-quality output — commercial copywriting, complex code — the gap between free and paid is noticeable.

**3. You need team collaboration**: Most free tiers are for individual use only. Shared workspaces, permission management, and team features typically require a paid plan.

**4. You have data privacy requirements**: Enterprise-grade data privacy protection is usually only available on paid plans. Keep this in mind when handling sensitive business information.

**5. You need API access**: Most tools in this guide only offer free access through the web interface. If you need to integrate them into your own products via API, you'll generally need to pay.

A practical rule of thumb: **if a tool saves you more than two hours per week, the paid version is usually worth it.**

## FAQ

**Q1: Do these free tools really not require a credit card?**

All the domestic Chinese tools in this guide (DeepSeek, Doubao, Kimi, Tongyi Qianwen, Jimeng AI, CodeGeeX, iFlytek) only require a phone number to register — no credit card needed. International tools (Gemini, Perplexity, NotebookLM, Codeium, Ideogram, Canva) require a Google account or email address, but also no credit card.

**Q2: Is my data safe with free tools?**

Free tools often use conversation data for model training (policies vary by provider). When handling sensitive information, it's a good idea to: ① read each tool's privacy policy; ② avoid inputting real personal data or trade secrets; ③ consider locally deployed options like Stable Diffusion or open-source models.

**Q3: Which tools can I use in China without a VPN?**

The following completely free tools work without a VPN: DeepSeek, Doubao, Kimi, Tongyi Qianwen, Jimeng AI, CodeGeeX, iFlytek, and CapCut (剪映). These eight tools cover the main use cases — chat, image generation, coding, and productivity — so users in China can build a complete free AI stack without needing to bypass restrictions.

**Q4: Which is better, DeepSeek or Doubao?**

They serve different purposes. DeepSeek is stronger for logical reasoning, code generation, and long-form writing. Doubao is better for casual conversation, multimodal interaction, and mobile use. We'd recommend registering for both and switching based on the task.

**Q5: Is the image quality from free AI image tools good enough?**

For social media visuals and content creation, the free tiers of Jimeng AI and Ideogram are absolutely sufficient. If you need commercial-grade quality or need to generate images in bulk, consider locally deploying Stable Diffusion (requires a GPU) or upgrading to a paid plan.

**Q6: Could these free policies change?**

Yes, they could. AI tool free tiers can be adjusted at any time. The data in this guide was verified on 2026-04-20. We recommend bookmarking this page and checking back for updates. Historically, ChatGPT has tightened its free tier and Midjourney dropped its free trial — but over the same period, more domestic tools have joined the free tier ecosystem, so the overall landscape of free tools continues to expand.

**Q7: Is there one tool that can replace all of these?**

Not yet. Each tool has a clear edge in its specific use case, and combining them is what gets you the most value. If you could only pick one, DeepSeek has the strongest all-around capability and covers the widest range of use cases — it's the top single-tool choice.
